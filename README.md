## 🛡️ Cyber-Safety Prevention Checklist
Derived from my research on digital security and common scams:

1.  **Secure UPI**: Never enter your UPI PIN to receive money; scammers use "Request Money" to trick victims.
2.  **Verify Recipients**: Confirm the recipient's name within the app before making any payment.
3.  **Enable 2FA**: Use an authenticator app (rather than SMS) for university email and social media accounts.
4.  **Audit Permissions**: Regularly check app access to your contacts, gallery, and microphone.
5.  **Identify Deepfakes**: If a friend or family member asks for urgent money via video/voice, call them back on their regular number to verify.
6.  **Avoid Public Wi-Fi**: Use mobile data or a trusted VPN for sensitive forms, fees, or banking portals.
7.  **Job Skepticism**: Legitimate companies will never ask for a "security deposit" or "registration fee" to hire you.
8.  **Profile Security**: Lock your Facebook profile and set social media to "Private" to prevent catfishing or identity theft.

> **Report Cybercrime**: Immediately report incidents at **cybercrime.gov.in** or call the national helpline **1930**.

## 🏥 Case Study: Ransomware on "MediCenter Health"
Analysis of a typical ransomware attack progression:
* **Infiltration**: An employee clicks a malicious link in an "Urgent Invoice" email, installing a Trojan backdoor.
* **Reconnaissance**: Attackers identify high-value targets like patient databases and backup servers.
* **Deployment**: Strong encryption algorithms (like **AES-256**) are used to lock files simultaneously across the network.
* **Impact**: Total halt of operations, postponed surgeries, diverted ambulances, and massive legal fines for data breaches.
